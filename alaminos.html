Explore the beauty of Hundred Islands

Alaminos used to be a part of Bolinao which was one of the nine towns in the northern most part of Zambales. It became a town independent of its mother town in 1747. The town got its name in 1872 in honor of Lieutenant Governor General Alaminos, the then Governor General of the Philippines.
The town became a city in 2001 by virtue of Republic Act 9025, An Act Converting the Municipality of Alaminos, Province of Pangasinan into a Component City to be known as the City of Alaminos.
Alaminos is the home to the Hundred Islands National Park, the very first national park in the Philippines. It is also the heart of Western Pangasinan, its center of commerce, finance, education, industry and services.

How to get there

To get to Alaminos from Manila, you can take a bus from terminals in Cubao or Pasay City, drive via the North Luzon Expressway and Tarlac-Pangasinan-La Union Expressway, or opt for private hire, with travel times ranging from 4 to 6 hours depending on the mode of transportation and traffic conditions. Upon arrival, tricycles and jeepneys are available for local transportation within Alaminos and nearby areas.

How much is the Fair

Bus fares from Manila to Alaminos typically range from approximately PHP 400 to PHP 700, depending on the bus company and the type of service you choose. For private hire or car rental, the cost will vary based on the type of vehicle and the duration of the rental. Toll fees for using the North Luzon Expressway (NLEX) and Tarlac-Pangasinan-La Union Expressway (TPLEX) would total around PHP 500-700 one way, plus fuel costs if you're driving your own vehicle.
