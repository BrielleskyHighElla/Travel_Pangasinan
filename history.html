History of Pangasinan

District 1 to 6

Pangasinan, a province in the Philippines has long history extending back to pre-colonial times. It was formerly inhabited by the Pangasinan people, who had their own unique culture and language. It belonged to the ancient kingdom of Pangasinan in the fifteenth century

Bani

Spanish Colonial Era: Spain governed Bani from 1521 to 1899. The Spanish authorities appointed the pueblo's Gobernadorcillos, while the Americans appointed the Presidentes from 1901 to 1936.

Lingayen

Lingayen was founded in 1614 after Augustinian and Spanish conquistadors drew a plan to establish the town. Lingayen was established as the capital of Pangasinan when the province became an encomienda.

Calasiao

The Municipal of Calasiao is the second oldest town in the Province of Pangasinan. It was founded by the Dominicians in year 1588.

Manaoag

The history of Manaoag began in the early 1600s as a small settlement called Cawili, located in what is now known as the town of San Jacinto.

Urdaneta City

Urdaneta City was founded on January 8, 1858, by Pangasinenses and Ilocanos who sought greater ties and unity. The city was named after Father Andrés de Urdaneta.

Asingan

The name Asingan was given by the Spaniards who arrived in the village and found the locals to be hostile and antagonistic towards them.
